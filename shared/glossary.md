# Canonical Shared Glossary

This glossary defines shared orientation terms used across Integrity Nexus and connected repositories.

The definitions are intentionally meta-level definitions.

Repository-specific definitions may be stricter or more technical inside TIG, SIR, SSC, or future repositories.

---

# Integrity

Integrity is the preservation of required structural conditions across system evolution.

Domain projections:

- TIG: geometric or gravitational structural integrity.
- SIR: recursive research and governance integrity.
- SSC: control-boundary and state-admissibility integrity.

---

# Bounded Structural Evolution

Bounded structural evolution is system evolution constrained by admissibility conditions, invariants, boundaries, or governance rules.

It is the common denominator across the connected repositories.

---

# Admissibility

Admissibility is the condition that a state, transition, structure, sector, claim, or dependency is permitted under the relevant domain rules.

---

# Boundary

A boundary separates admissible from non-admissible states, transitions, sectors, claims, or dependencies.

---

# Constraint

A constraint is a rule or condition that restricts possible evolution, interpretation, transition, or claim expansion.

---

# Invariant

An invariant is a condition intended to remain preserved across allowed evolution.

---

# Recursion

Recursion is a process in which a system, rule, structure, or governance layer refers back to itself or produces further instances of its own structure.

---

# Controller

A controller is a component, structure, or process that restricts, evaluates, or regulates system evolution.

In SSC, this term has a technical control-boundary meaning.

---

# Supervisor

A supervisor is a layer that observes, evaluates, or authorizes another layer.

Integrity Nexus treats supervisor dependency as a cross-domain governance issue when recursive validation becomes relevant.

---

# Verifier

A verifier is a component or process that checks whether a state, transition, proof object, or claim satisfies registered conditions.

---

# Enforcement

Enforcement is the mechanism by which a rejected transition, claim, or dependency is actually prevented from becoming active.

---

# Governance

Governance is the set of rules that preserves research discipline, claim boundaries, dependency clarity, and publication readiness.

---

# Claim Boundary

A claim boundary defines the maximum strength with which a claim may be stated given its current evidence, proof status, and dependency status.

---

# Dependency

A dependency is a required relationship between concepts, claims, proofs, repositories, or governance structures.

---

# System of Record

A system of record is the canonical location where status, standards, and authoritative cross-repository mappings are maintained.

Integrity Nexus acts as the system of record for cross-repository governance and navigation.
