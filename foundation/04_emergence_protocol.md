# Emergence Protocol

## Locked Mode Scope

This document defines the controlled emergence protocol for TIG-E.

It does not create theory, prove results, or promote candidates into canonical status.

## Purpose

The Emergence Protocol defines how TIG-E prepares conditions under which new candidate structures may arise from a compressed and governed search space.

Emergence is not treated as magic, intuition, or uncontrolled invention. It is treated as a structured result of constrained candidate interaction under governance.

## Definition

```text
Controlled Emergence = the appearance of new candidate structures after invalid, circular, non-fundamental, unsupported, or misplaced paths have been removed or marked within the search space.
```

## What Emergence Is Not

Emergence in TIG-E is not:

- an automatic proof,
- a final truth claim,
- a guarantee of correctness,
- a replacement for review,
- a license to invent unsupported theory,
- or a shortcut around dependency resolution.

## What Emergence Does

Emergence allows the system to propose new candidate structures when:

1. the claim has been structured,
2. assumptions have been made explicit,
3. candidates have been classified,
4. invalid or misplaced structures have been marked,
5. dependencies have been mapped,
6. the search space has been compressed,
7. and the remaining candidate space is sufficiently constrained for reviewable proposal generation.

The result is not final truth.

The result is a **reviewable candidate**.

## Emergence Cycle

```text
Input
  ↓
Structuring
  ↓
Fundamentality Filter
  ↓
Integrity Filter
  ↓
Candidate Registry
  ↓
Search Space Compression
  ↓
Emergent Candidate Proposal
  ↓
Review Queue
  ↓
Decision / Registry Update
```

## Governance Rule

```text
Emergent outputs must remain candidates until reviewed, registered, and justified.
```

No emergent output may be promoted directly into canonical status.

## Difference Between Generation and Discovery

TIG-E does not claim to create truth.

It creates structured conditions under which a smaller, more coherent candidate space can reveal candidate structures that were previously hidden by noise, level errors, unresolved dependencies, or search-space overload.

## Relation to Search Space Compression

Search Space Compression prepares emergence.

Emergence is permitted only after the system has reduced or clarified the search space enough to make the proposal reviewable.

```text
Compression before emergence.
Review before canonization.
```

## Domain Neutrality

In physics, an emergent candidate may be a candidate operator, field-equation structure, or relation between fundamental variables.

In medicine, it may be a mechanism linking multiple symptoms.

In product development, it may be a core feature architecture.

In AI alignment, it may be a governance constraint.

The domain differs. The emergence protocol remains the same.

## Locked Mode Constraint

```text
Candidate ≠ final.
Emergent ≠ proven.
Proposal ≠ canon.
```

Every emergent structure remains subject to registry classification, dependency review, and explicit human decision.
