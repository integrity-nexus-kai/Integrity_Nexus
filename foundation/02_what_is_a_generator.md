# What is a Generator?

## Definition

A **generator** is the deepest currently identified mechanism from which a class of observable structures, states, behaviors, or explanations emerges.

A generator is not merely a cause in a local chain. It is the structural source from which a set of projections becomes intelligible.

## Projection

A **projection** is an observable or model-level expression of a deeper mechanism.

A projection may be valid, useful, measurable, and necessary inside a domain, while still being non-fundamental relative to a deeper generator.

## Core Distinction

```text
Generator = mechanism that produces structure.
Projection = structure produced by a mechanism.
```

TIG-E treats this distinction as a primary governance rule.

## Explanation Depth Criterion

An explanation is not considered complete merely because it is plausible, elegant, coherent, or locally useful.

It remains methodically incomplete if it can still be understood as the projection of a deeper mechanism.

```text
A sufficient explanation is one that has reached the deepest currently available generator level.
```

This does not mean that TIG-E claims final metaphysical depth. It means that each explanation must be tested against the question:

```text
Is this already the generator, or is it still a projection?
```

## Methodological Role

The generator concept prevents premature closure.

It protects the research process from accepting:

- symptoms as causes,
- domain artifacts as foundations,
- convenient variables as primitives,
- local models as universal explanations,
- and emergent descriptions as fundamental structures.

## Domain Neutrality

The generator concept is domain-neutral.

In physics, a generator may relate to structures below spacetime.

In medicine, a generator may be a biological mechanism below multiple symptoms.

In AI alignment, a generator may be a governance principle below observed model behavior.

In product strategy, a generator may be the core user need below visible feature requests.

The domain changes. The generator/projection test remains.

## Abbruchbedingung / Stop Condition

TIG-E does not stop when an answer sounds correct.

It stops only provisionally when the current explanation cannot, under the available model and evidence, be reduced to a deeper generator without introducing unsupported assumptions.

This stop condition is provisional and auditable.
