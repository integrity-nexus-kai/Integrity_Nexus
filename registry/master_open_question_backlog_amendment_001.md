## OQ-033 — Dynamic Preservation of Global Consistency

**Local Registry ID:** SFQ-NEXUS-001  
**Question Class:** OQ-SF / OQ-MAT / OQ-MET  
**Question State:** OPEN  
**Registry Status:** Registered  
**Scientific Status Applicability:** APPLICABLE  
**Scientific Status:** Scientifically Open  
**Claim Status:** No value assigned  
**Ownership Scope:** Shared research frontier  

### Problem

How can a globally constrained structural state be updated dynamically and discretely without losing global consistency during the transition itself?

### Participating Research Positions

- Koncsik emergence hypotheses, based on unpublished manuscripts supplied for private scientific review;
- TIG/TIG-E working structures, subject to canonical repository evidence and claim boundaries.

### Current Blocker

No source-validated, mathematically explicit transition law currently bridges static/global compatibility conditions and discrete dynamical updating while proving preservation during every transition state.

### Required Work

- source-exact comparative audit;
- external permission control;
- candidate mathematical-object inventory;
- dynamic preservation theorem or equivalent validation target;
- experimental consequence derivation and discrimination criteria;
- downstream gate registration before scientific execution.

### Resolution Criterion

A mathematically explicit update law must be derived and its preservation properties established under a defined framework. Any proposed experimental consequence must follow from that law and be distinguishable from environmental or technical noise.

### Claim Boundary

This entry does not establish theory equivalence, collaboration, endorsement, co-authorship, publication permission, a completed bridge, discrete action as fact, or sheaf cohomology as a selected TIG formalism.
