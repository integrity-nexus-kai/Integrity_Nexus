## OQ-033 — Dynamic Preservation of Global Consistency

**Local Registry ID:** SFQ-NEXUS-001  
**Question Class:** OQ-SF / OQ-MAT / OQ-MET  
**Question State:** OPEN  
**Registry Status:** Registered  
**Scientific Status Applicability:** APPLICABLE  
**Scientific Status:** Scientifically Open  
**Claim Status:** No value assigned  
**Ownership Scope:** Shared research frontier  

### Problem

How can a globally constrained structural state be updated dynamically and discretely without losing global consistency during the transition itself?

### Participating Research Positions

- Koncsik emergence hypotheses, based on unpublished manuscripts supplied for private scientific review;
- TIG/TIG-E working structures, subject to canonical repository evidence and claim boundaries.

### Current Blocker

No source-validated, mathematically explicit transition law currently bridges static/global compatibility conditions and discrete dynamical updating while proving preservation during every transition state.

### Required Work

- source-exact comparative audit;
- external permission control;
- candidate mathematical-object inventory;
- dynamic preservation theorem or equivalent validation target;
- experimental consequence derivation and discrimination criteria;
- downstream gate registration before scientific execution.

### Resolution Criterion

A mathematically explicit update law must be derived and its preservation properties established under a defined framework. Any proposed experimental consequence must follow from that law and be distinguishable from environmental or technical noise.

### Claim Boundary

This entry does not establish theory equivalence, collaboration, endorsement, co-authorship, publication permission, a completed bridge, discrete action as fact, or sheaf cohomology as a selected TIG formalism.

## OQ-034 — Operational Projection of Topological Consistency onto a Discrete Cube Architecture

**Local Registry ID:** SFQ-NEXUS-002  
**Question Class:** OQ-SF / OQ-MET / OQ-EXP  
**Question State:** OPEN  
**Registry Status:** Registered  
**Scientific Status Applicability:** APPLICABLE  
**Scientific Status:** Scientifically Open  
**Claim Status:** No value assigned  
**Ownership Scope:** Shared research frontier  

### Problem

How can topological or global consistency conditions be operationally represented, simulated, and experimentally investigated within a discrete three-dimensional Cube architecture without assuming that the Cube architecture itself is already the correct physical substrate?

### Participating Research Positions

- Koncsik emergence hypotheses, based on unpublished manuscripts supplied for private scientific review;
- TIG/TIG-E Cube research, subject to canonical maturity, ontology, state-space, and claim boundaries.

### Dependency Relation

OQ-034 is related to OQ-033 but is not a duplicate.

```text
OQ-033 asks for the mathematical law preserving global consistency during discrete transition.
OQ-034 asks how consistency conditions can be mapped, simulated, and discriminated within a Cube architecture.
```

Neither entry resolves the other.

### Current Blocker

No source-validated mapping currently connects external topological/global consistency conditions to canonical Cube variables, update rules, validation conditions, observables, and falsification criteria.

### Required Work

- source-exact consistency-condition extraction;
- canonical Cube state and transition inventory;
- framework-neutral mapping definition;
- reproducible simulation architecture;
- local and global observable definition;
- null-model and numerical-artifact controls;
- physical-interpretation bridge separation;
- downstream gate registration before execution.

### Resolution Criterion

A source-valid mapping must be explicitly defined and implemented in a reproducible Cube simulation or equivalent formal model. Success, failure, and null criteria must be fixed in advance. Simulation output may receive a physical interpretation only through a separately derived and audited bridge.

### Claim Boundary

This entry does not establish that Cubes are the physical substrate of spacetime, that a three-dimensional Cube lattice is equivalent to Koncsik's two-time surface construction, that the participating frameworks are mathematically equivalent, that an experiment is feasible, or that simulation output constitutes empirical evidence.
