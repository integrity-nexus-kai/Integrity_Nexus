# Shared Concepts

This document records concepts shared across multiple Integrity Nexus repositories.

Shared concepts do not mean identical technical definitions in every domain.

They provide cross-repository orientation.

---

# Integrity

Integrity means preservation of required structural conditions across system evolution.

Domain projections:

- TIG: geometric / gravitational structural integrity.
- SIR: recursive integrity across research and governance structures.
- SSC: control-boundary and state-admissibility integrity.

---

# Bounded Evolution

Bounded evolution means that system change is constrained by admissibility, invariants, or structural limits.

Domain projections:

- TIG: bounded gravitational transitions.
- SIR: bounded recursive governance evolution.
- SSC: bounded state transitions.

---

# Admissibility

Admissibility means that a state, transition, structure, or sector is permitted under the relevant domain constraints.

Domain projections:

- TIG: admissible geometric or horizon-sector structures.
- SIR: admissible recursive research-state transitions.
- SSC: admissible system states and transitions.

---

# Boundary

A boundary is a structural distinction between admissible and non-admissible regions, states, claims, or transitions.

Domain projections:

- TIG: physical/geometric boundary conditions.
- SIR: governance and dependency boundaries.
- SSC: control and capability boundaries.

---

# Invariant

An invariant is a condition intended to remain preserved across allowed evolution.

Domain projections:

- TIG: mathematical or geometric preservation conditions.
- SIR: recursive integrity conditions.
- SSC: control invariants over state transitions.
