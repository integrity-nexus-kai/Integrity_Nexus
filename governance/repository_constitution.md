# Repository Constitution

This document defines the constitutional principles of Integrity Nexus.

It is the highest-level governance document for the meta-repository.

---

# Mission

Integrity Nexus exists to make an integrity-oriented multi-repository research ecosystem navigable, governable, and coherent.

It connects repositories without replacing them.

It preserves domain separation while making shared structures visible.

---

# Core Function

Integrity Nexus provides:

- navigation,
- shared terminology,
- repository status tracking,
- maturity classification,
- cross-repository dependency mapping,
- claim-boundary discipline,
- citation discipline,
- and governance standards.

---

# Non-Goals

Integrity Nexus does not:

- introduce a new scientific theory,
- prove the claims of connected repositories,
- merge all repositories into one domain,
- convert analogies into formal dependencies,
- replace repository-specific governance,
- or inflate exploratory claims into results.

---

# Claim Policy

All cross-repository claims must be classified.

Allowed classes include:

- navigation reference,
- shared terminology,
- conceptual analogy,
- governance dependency,
- evidence-supported dependency,
- formal dependency.

A conceptual analogy must never be stated as a proof.

---

# Evidence Policy

Evidence must remain separated from interpretation.

No relationship may be promoted beyond its evidence class.

External evidence should be cited in the relevant repository, not only in Integrity Nexus.

---

# Publication Policy

Integrity Nexus may be cited as a governance and navigation artifact.

It should not be cited as the source of domain-specific technical results unless those results are explicitly developed inside Integrity Nexus.

---

# Governance Principles

1. Domain separation before synthesis.
2. Claim boundaries before public positioning.
3. Evidence before escalation.
4. Registry before canonization.
5. Maturity classification before publication claims.
6. Navigation before interpretation.
7. Coherence without collapse.

---

# Authority

Integrity Nexus is authoritative for cross-repository navigation, status, and governance standards.

It is not authoritative over domain-specific truth claims inside connected repositories.

Those must be established inside the relevant repository.
