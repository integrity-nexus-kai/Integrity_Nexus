## Purpose

The TIG Research Ecosystem must distinguish unresolved scientific content by question type and ownership scope.

A generic open-question list is insufficient because mathematical, methodological, experimental, and cross-framework questions require different evidence, resolution, and collaboration controls.

This governance document introduces four non-exclusive classes:

```text
OQ-MAT — Mathematical Open Question
OQ-MET — Methodological Open Question
OQ-EXP — Experimental / Computational Open Question
OQ-SF  — Shared Frontier Question
```

Question class is governance metadata. It does not replace Question State, Registry Status, Scientific Status Applicability, Scientific Status, Claim Status, Operational Status, maturity, dependency, bridge, evidence, or completion-readiness controls.

---

## OQ-MAT — Mathematical Open Question

Resolution primarily requires formal definition, proof, derivation, operator construction, theorem, closure result, consistency result, or limit analysis.

## OQ-MET — Methodological Open Question

Resolution primarily concerns scientific method, framework comparison, operationalization, terminology, audit design, validation, evidence separation, or research workflow.

## OQ-EXP — Experimental / Computational Open Question

Resolution primarily depends on observation, measurement, simulation, numerical analysis, falsification, or empirical discrimination.

## OQ-SF — Shared Frontier Question

A question located at the interface between independent research programs where no single framework currently possesses a complete solution.

Shared Frontier Questions are research-frontier objects rather than ownership claims.

---

## Shared Frontier Controls

A Shared Frontier Question must:

1. preserve the internal semantics of all participating frameworks;
2. distinguish structural correspondence from mathematical identity;
3. cite source-exact positions for every participating framework;
4. state the collaboration and permission status explicitly;
5. avoid importing unpublished external material without permission;
6. define forbidden inferences;
7. identify mathematical, methodological, and experimental subquestions separately;
8. define a resolution criterion;
9. retain all canonical claim and scientific-status boundaries.

The following non-identities are mandatory:

```text
shared question != shared answer
shared frontier != formal collaboration
structural correspondence != theory equivalence
external manuscript access != publication permission
registry admission != evidence
methodological proposal != scientific derivation
```

## Initial Shared Frontier Registration Rule

The first registered frontier may concern the operational emergence interface identified between Koncsik's emergence hypotheses and TIG/TIG-E working structures:

```text
How can a globally constrained structural state be updated dynamically and discretely without losing global consistency during the transition itself?
```

This question may be registered as an open shared frontier only.

It must not be represented as:

- an established collaboration,
- a jointly accepted formulation,
- a completed bridge,
- a proof of discrete action,
- a proof of sheaf-cohomological dynamics,
- a validation of TIG,
- or permission to publish external unpublished manuscripts.

## Repository Distribution

Local repositories register domain-specific projections.

Integrity Nexus maintains:

- the cross-repository classification,
- shared-frontier relations,
- cross-ID mappings,
- permission and boundary metadata,
- and master-backlog synchronization.

No Shared Frontier registration changes the canonical scientific status of a local repository object.
