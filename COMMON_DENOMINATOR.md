# Common Denominator

The connected repositories in the Integrity Nexus ecosystem do not study the same object in the same domain.

They share a structural research principle.

---

# Shared Principle

```text
Integrity-preserving bounded structural evolution
```

This is the common denominator across TIG, SIR, SSC, and future integrity-oriented research programs.

---

# Meaning

A system evolves.

Its evolution may be physical, recursive, informational, mathematical, organizational, or control-theoretic.

Integrity is preserved when this evolution is not arbitrary, but constrained by admissibility conditions, structural boundaries, invariants, or governance rules.

---

# Domain Projections

## TIG

TIG investigates bounded structural evolution in gravitational and geometric contexts.

## SIR

SIR investigates bounded recursive evolution in integrity-preserving research and governance structures.

## SSC

SSC investigates bounded state evolution in control architectures and AI-control systems.

---

# What This Does Not Mean

The common denominator does not imply that all repositories make the same claims.

It does not merge physics, governance, and AI control into one undifferentiated theory.

It identifies a shared structural pattern while preserving domain boundaries.

---

# Operational Translation

Across the ecosystem, the recurring question is:

```text
Which transitions are admissible, and which boundaries preserve integrity?
```

Each repository answers this question in its own domain.
