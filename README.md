# Integrity Nexus

Integrity Nexus is a meta-repository for research governance, dependency mapping, and cross-repository integrity architecture.

It does not replace the underlying research repositories.

It provides navigation, conceptual alignment, dependency orientation, and shared governance standards across the integrity-oriented research ecosystem.

---

# Purpose

Integrity Nexus answers three questions:

1. Which research repositories exist?
2. How do they relate to each other?
3. What is their shared structural denominator?

---

# Common Denominator

Across the connected repositories, the central research object is:

```text
Integrity-preserving bounded structural evolution
```

This means that each repository studies a different domain in which system evolution is constrained by admissibility, boundary conditions, invariants, or structural integrity principles.

---

# Connected Research Programs

## TIG — Topological Integrity Gravity

TIG studies bounded structural transitions in gravitational and geometric settings.

## SIR — Structural Integrity Recursion

SIR studies recursive integrity, constraint navigation, and governance of recursive research structures.

## SSC — Structural State Controller

SSC studies supervisor-independent control boundaries, state admissibility, and invariant-preserving AI-control architectures.

---

# Repository Role

Integrity Nexus is not a new theory layer.

It is a navigation and coherence layer.

Its role is to:

- map repository relationships,
- define shared terminology,
- maintain cross-repository governance standards,
- identify shared dependencies,
- preserve claim boundaries,
- and make the full research ecosystem readable for human reviewers and automated research-audit systems.
